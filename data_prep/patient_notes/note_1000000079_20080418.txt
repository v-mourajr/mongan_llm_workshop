This is a 33-year-old Asian female who primarily speaks Spanish and has been under our care for several years. She has a known history of asthma and has had multiple follow-up visits to manage this condition. Today, she presents for a routine follow-up visit.

**Subjective:**
The patient reports an exacerbation of asthma symptoms over the past few weeks, including increased wheezing, shortness of breath, and chest tightness. She notes that exposure to cold air and dust significantly aggravates her symptoms. Despite regular use of her daily corticosteroid inhaler, she has had to use her rescue inhaler more frequently. 

In addition to her respiratory complaints, the patient reports episodes of dysuria with symptoms such as burning sensations during urination and darker urine. There is no accompanying abdominal pain or fever. She recalls having recurrent urinary tract infections (UTIs) in the past. The patient denies any recent surgical procedures.

Her current medications include albuterol sulfate 4 mg tablets as needed, Zyrtec syrup for allergic rhinitis, and Zantac solution for esophageal reflux. She adheres to her medication regimen.

**Assessment:**
1. **Asthma:** The patient is experiencing a moderate exacerbation of her chronic asthma, likely triggered by exposure to cold air and dust.
2. **Urinary Tract Infection (UTI):** Symptoms of dysuria and the abnormal urinalysis findings strongly suggest a UTI.

**Plan:**
1. **Asthma Management:**
   - Continue maintenance therapy with the corticosteroid inhaler.
   - Increase albuterol use as needed, with careful monitoring of frequency.
   - If no improvement in symptoms within one week, consider a short course of oral corticosteroids.
   - Provide patient education on asthma triggers and inhaler technique.

2. **UTI Management:**
   - Start a course of oral antibiotics tailored to her symptoms and history of recurrent UTIs.
   - Increase fluid intake to help clear the infection.
   - Schedule a follow-up urinalysis after completing antibiotics to ensure infection resolution.

**Follow-Up:**
- Instruct the patient to return to the clinic if her asthma symptoms worsen despite increased medication use or if new symptoms arise.
- A follow-up appointment is scheduled in one week to reassess asthma control and evaluate the response to UTI treatment.
