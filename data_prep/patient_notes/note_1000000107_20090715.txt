This is a 31-year-old Hispanic male who has been under care at our clinic for approximately twelve years. He speaks German and presents today for a follow-up visit. The patient reports intermittent abdominal discomfort and loose stools, which have increased in both frequency and severity over the last week. Additionally, he has noticed a reappearance of a generalized erythematous rash. He denies any recent fever, chills, night sweats, or respiratory symptoms.

The patient's relevant past medical history includes episodes of recurrent noninfectious gastroenteritis, unspecified upper respiratory tract infections, developmental learning difficulties, a history of scarlet fever in childhood, a wrist sprain, and an aneurysm of the heart. He has been utilizing over-the-counter antacids to manage his abdominal symptoms and topical antihistamines for his rash, though he reports that these measures provide only partial relief.

The patient is currently a postgraduate student living in a shared apartment. He maintains an active lifestyle as a member of his university's soccer team and abstains from smoking, alcohol, and recreational drugs. He has a known allergy to penicillin, which causes hives.

The patient's abdominal pain is described as a dull, crampy sensation localized in the lower abdomen, aggravated by food intake and somewhat alleviated by antacids. The erythematous rash is pruritic and located on his chest and back; it has been severe during previous episodes of scarlet fever.

### Plan:

- Prescribe a short course of low-potency corticosteroid cream for better symptomatic relief.

- Follow up in dermatology if there is no improvement in the condition.

#### 3. Developmental Learning Difficulties:

- Refer the patient to a learning specialist for comprehensive assessment and support.
