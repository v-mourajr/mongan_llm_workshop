On 08/22/2001, a 55-year-old Indian female patient presented to the clinic for a follow-up appointment to manage her asthma symptoms and ongoing gynecological conditions. The patient has been receiving care at our facility over the past few years for a range of health issues. She is fluent in English and has a history of following treatment plans diligently. 

The primary reason for today's visit is to evaluate her asthma, which has been managed with an albuterol inhaler. She reports experiencing increased coughing episodes at night, leading to disturbed sleep. She denies any recent upper respiratory infections or use of additional home remedies beyond a humidifier, which provides marginal relief. 

She has a significant past medical history involving cervicitis and endocervicitis, endometriosis of the uterus, leiomyomas, and hypopotassemia. The patient has undergone a total abdominal hysterectomy and other proceedings for uterine issues. Her current medication regimen includes albuterol as a rescue inhaler and a potassium supplement. She has adhered well to these medications.

Socially, she is a non-smoker, does not consume alcohol, and lives a relatively sedentary lifestyle due to her health issues. She is married with two grown children and maintains employment in a sedentary job. 

During today's assessment, the patient denied any fever or significant weight changes but mentioned mild abdominal discomfort possibly related to her gynecological conditions. She finds the humidifier to be somewhat helpful at night for her asthma symptoms.
